The National Institute of Technology, Warangal (NIT Warangal or NITW) is a public technological university located in Warangal, India. It is recognised as an Institute of National Importance by the Government of India. The foundation stone for this institute was laid by then Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru on 1959, the first in the chain of 32 NITs (formerly known as RECs) in the country. The institute was renamed as the National Institute of Technology, Warangal in 2002.
The campus is located in Warangal, spread over an area of 256 acres
